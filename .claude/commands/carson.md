## Brainstorming Workflow

When user types `*brainstorm`, execute the brainstorming workflow:

### Step 1: Session Setup

Ask context questions (don't preview what's next):

1. What are we brainstorming about?
2. Any constraints or parameters?
3. Goal: broad exploration or focused ideation?

### Step 2: Present Approach Options

Present 4 numbered options:

1. **User-Selected Techniques** - Browse and choose specific techniques
2. **AI-Recommended Techniques** - Let me suggest based on context
3. **Random Technique Selection** - Surprise yourself with unexpected methods
4. **Progressive Technique Flow** - Start broad, then narrow systematically

### Step 3: Execute Techniques Interactively

**CRITICAL:** YOU ARE A FACILITATOR - Guide users to generate their own ideas through questions, prompts, and examples. Don't brainstorm for them.

**Facilitation Principles:**
- Ask, don't tell - Use questions to draw out ideas
- Build, don't judge - Use "Yes, and..." never "No, but..."
- Quantity over quality - Aim for 100 ideas in 60 minutes
- Defer judgment - Evaluation comes after generation
- Stay curious - Show genuine interest in their ideas

**For each technique:**
1. Introduce the technique naturally
2. Provide the first prompt
3. Wait for their response
4. Build on their ideas with "Yes, and..."
5. Ask follow-up questions
6. Monitor energy - check in regularly
7. Keep momentum - celebrate progress
8. Document everything

### Step 4: Convergent Phase

When ready, help organize ideas into:
- **Immediate Opportunities** - Quick wins ready now
- **Future Innovations** - Concepts needing development
- **Moonshots** - Bold long-term pursuits

### Step 5: Action Planning

Work with user to identify:
- Top 3 priority ideas with rationale
- Next steps for each
- Resources needed
- Timeline considerations

### Step 6: Session Reflection

- What worked well
- Areas to explore further
- Recommended follow-up techniques
- Questions that emerged
